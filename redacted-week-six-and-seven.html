Week Nine

Midterms, Mind Maps & Minor Breakdowns
Date: 10.03.2025 - 22.02.2025

I’m combining these two weeks because — let’s be real — midterms hit like a truck and I barely opened Unity except to check if my buttons still clicked. (They didn’t. Of course.)

🧠 Brainstorming in the Background

Even though my actual development slowed to a crawl, my brain didn’t stop running laps. I found myself sketching scene layouts during lectures, mapping out puzzle logic on the back of flashcards, and trying to mentally untangle our game’s act structure during library breaks.

I also made a mood board in Notion (read: I dumped like 40 screenshots of Slay the Princess and creepy tower architecture into a page and called it research). Visual vibes are solid. Execution? TBD.

📉 Development Lull = Design Recovery

This wasn’t a totally wasted stretch, though. I started to seriously re-evaluate our UI structure — especially how we were handling dialogue and transitions. I’d been hardcoding too much, and it was becoming unsustainable. These weeks helped me realize I needed to rebuild it more modularly — and after midterms, that’s exactly what I started doing.

😮‍💨 Real Talk: Burnout Warning Signs

Midway through Week 7 I hit a classic student-dev wall. I was mentally exhausted, juggling research deadlines with group projects, and struggling with the guilt of not doing more. But weirdly? That pause helped. I was able to come back with clearer eyes and better structure the chaos that was to come in Week 8.

📝 What Worked:

  • Sketching puzzles while pretending to study for midterms
  • Building visual tone boards for future scenes
  • Reimagining the dialogue manager in theory before rebuilding it in practice

💥 What Didn’t:

  • Unity. I barely opened it.
  • Time management. Cramming and devving are mortal enemies.
  • Sleep. Let’s just say I owe myself 12 naps.

Next up: the glittery collapse that was Week 8, where I learned that particles are not to be trusted and nothing — nothing — is ever layered the way you think it is.
