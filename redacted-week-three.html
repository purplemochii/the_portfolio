Week Three

Slow Progress, but Progress Nonetheless
Date: 10.02.2025 - 15.02.2025

This week was quieter than expected, with more discussions than actual development.
However, I still managed to make some progress, especially in solidifying my C# knowledge.

Finalizing Game Mechanics

Most of this week was spent in discussions with my team. We refined how core mechanics like interactions, puzzles, and the painting system would function in the final game. While no actual coding happened, this step was crucial for preventing confusion later.

C# Tutorials: Finally Done!

I wrapped up my W3Schools C# tutorials, which covered important Unity-relevant concepts like OOP, event handling, and collections. I haven't had the chance to apply this knowledge yet, but at least I now understand the syntax better.

A Quiet, Low-Energy Week

Honestly, I didn’t do much beyond the discussions and tutorials. Whether it was burnout or just a natural slowdown, I didn’t feel very productive. But I reminded myself that slow weeks happen, and as long as I stay consistent, I’ll bounce back.

What I Learned / My Takeaways

  • Team discussions are just as important as hands-on development.
  • C# syntax makes more sense now, but I still need practice.
  • Slow progress is better than no progress.

Goals for Next Week

  1. Get familiar with Unity’s UI system since I’ll be handling the game’s UI.
  2. Start experimenting with menus and interactive elements.
  3. Regain momentum and start building real, visible progress.

Conclusion: A Moment to Regroup

I wish I had done more this week, but at least the planning phase is wrapping up. Now, it’s time to shift from theory to action and start implementing the UI elements I’ll be responsible for.
