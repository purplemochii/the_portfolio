Week Five

Layers, Loss, and Learning the Truth About PNGs
Date: 3.03.2025 – 9.03.2025

This week was… intense. I started building the banquet scene properly, the first real puzzle scene in our game, and it was the first time I felt the full weight of designing a complex 2D-first-person scene in Unity. I knew it would be tricky, but I did not know it would chew me up and spit me out like this.

Let’s talk about layering. I had this vision of building a 2.5D parallax-style scene using world space canvases and layers of 960x405 PNGs that scrolled as the player moved the camera left and right. Simple enough in theory. I had all the assets, built a beautiful stack of panels—foreground elements, midground dancers, chandeliers, and sparkles—each perfectly composed in Photoshop.

And then I tried to make a button clickable. Nothing happened.

I assumed the script was broken. I checked the canvas, the event system, the raycasters, even the layers—everything looked fine. Unity said there were no errors. So why couldn’t I click the button sitting on top of the chandelier? I lost a solid two days spiraling over this before I had a 3am revelation after collapsing mid-debug:

All of my PNGs were full-sized 960x405 images. Even though only part of the image was “visible,” the rest of the transparent canvas was still blocking interactions below it.

So, yeah. All those lovely dancers? Blocking the button. The floating centerpiece? Blocking the mini-game. The literal sparkles? Blocking my sanity.

To fix this, I cropped every single screw and object to the size of their visible area and reimported all of them. I also restructured the entire hierarchy: separated all interactable objects into a dedicated “UI_Interactables” layer sitting above the decor, and made sure every UI element with a collider had Raycast Target enabled and a graphic raycaster on the canvas. Only then—only then—did things start to behave.

Successes this week:

  • Got horizontal camera movement working using input axes and clamped boundaries.
  • Implemented parallax scrolling between three layers.
  • Added an interactable button to launch the chandelier mini-game.
  • Realized I need to crop everything manually and not trust transparent PNGs ever again.

Things that drove me to madness:

  • Unity’s lack of error messages for UI layering conflicts.
  • Buttons being hidden behind "invisible" images.
  • Realizing halfway through testing that I had three EventSystems active. (Oops.)

Takeaway of the week:

Just because something looks layered properly doesn’t mean Unity knows it’s layered properly. Visual hierarchy ≠ interaction hierarchy. And if something’s not clickable? Check your raycasters. Check your canvas. Check your cropped assets. Then cry and try again.
